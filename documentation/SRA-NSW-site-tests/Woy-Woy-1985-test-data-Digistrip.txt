# This file holds the temperature data recorded on the freight train run through the Woy Woy tunnel in northern New
# South Wales on the morning of 28 April 1985.  The tests were conducted by the State Rail Authority (SRA) of NSW,
# Australia.
#
# Three uphill runs through the tunnel were made at different speeds and different loco notches.
# The front two locos were run at a given notch, the rear loco used its dynamic brake to load up the driving locos.
# The Woy Woy tunnel appears to be 1.79 km long, straight, with a 1 in 150 slope (data from the NSW government's
# Office of Environment & Heritage).
#
# For a full description of the tests, refer to the scan of the test results, "1985-Maldon-Pt-Kembla-Woy-Woy-test-results.pdf".
#
# The train consist was as follows (from the report):
#         Lead engine - 81 class                 - No. 2 end leading (condenser discharge in front of engine air intake)
#         Trailing engine - 8117                 - No. 1 end leading (engine air intake in front of condenser discharge)
#         Test Car - DMC 1902                    - Dynamometer car (DMC), coupled behind 8117
#         Train Load                             - 21 coal wagons (of unknown length)
#         Brake Van                              - also known as the guards van in the test results
#         Rear engine - 81 class
#
# The test recorded temperature data in front of the train, at points around the second engine and alongside the DMC.
# Data was taken every ten seconds on a 16-channel logger in the DMC, as follows:
#
# channel 101: Air temperature in front of the leading locomotive (not 8117, the one in front of it)
#
#         102: Water temperature out of the radiator on 8117
#         103: Water temperature into the radiator on 8117
#
#         104: Air temperature alongside DMC, 2 m above rail level, RH side next to empty track
#         105: Air temperature alongside DMC, 4 m above rail level, RH side
#         106: Air temperature alongside DMC, 2 m above rail level, LH side next to tunnel wall
#         107: Air temperature alongside DMC, 4 m above rail level, LH side
#
#         108: Air temperature out of dynavane on 8117 loco (air plus dust removed by an inertial filter)
#         109: Air temperature into dynavane left side on 8117 loco (intake air for engine next to tunnel wall)
#         110: Air temperature into dynavane right side on 8117 loco (intake air for engine next to empty track)
#
#         111: Air temperature into radiator right side No. 1 end (near nose of loco next to empty track)
#         112: Air temperature into radiator right side centre
#         113: Air temperature into radiator right side No. 2 end (near tail of loco next to empty track)
#
#         114: Air temperature into radiator left side No. 1 end (near nose of loco next to tunnel wall)
#         115: Air temperature into radiator left side centre
#         116: Air temperature into radiator left side No. 2 end (near tail of loco next to tunnel wall)
#
# Additional temperature data was recorded on a four-channel Honeywell logger at 20 second intervals aboard the
# brake van.  The four temperature sensors were on the outside of the brake van, 2 m and 4 m above rail level
# and on either side of the van.
# The data for these 4 channels are held in the files "Woy-Woy-1985-test-data-Honeywell.txt" and the plot
# files "Woy-Woy-1985-test-brake-van-TC1.txt", "Woy-Woy-1985-test-brake-van-TC2.txt",
# "Woy-Woy-1985-test-brake-van-TC3.txt" and "Woy-Woy-1985-test-brake-van-TC4.txt".
#
# Finally, air temperature data was recorded by a crew in the tunnel at the 68 km marker plate inside the
# tunnel, at 30 second intervals.  The data for this is held in the file "Woy-Woy-1985-test-68-km-post.txt"
#
